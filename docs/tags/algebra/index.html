Fibonacci numbers and determinant

November 29, 2023 (Last Modified: November 30, 2023)
Fibonacci sequence is a sequence as \[1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, 21, 34, \cdots,\] it satisfies: \(F_n = F_{n - 1} + F_{n - 2}(n \ge 3), F_1 = 1, F_2 = 2\) Proof the Fibonacci numbers \(F_n\) can be get from the determinant \[ F_n = {\begin{vmatrix} 1 & -1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0\\ 1 & 1 & -1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0\\ 0 & 1 & 1 & -1 & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 0\\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 & 1 & -1\\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 & 1 & 1\\ \end{vmatrix} }; \]

Exercises of determinant

November 26, 2023 (Last Modified: November 30, 2023)
Suppose \(n\ge 2\)，show that if the elements of a \(n \times n\) matrix \(A\) are 1 or -1, then \(|A|\) must be an even number. Consider the symmetries of \(a_{1i}\), \(a_{2j}\) and \(a_{1j}\), \(a_{2i}\), the value of corresponding determinant of the \(2 \times 2\) matrix must be an even number, so the n-order determinant must be an even number.
